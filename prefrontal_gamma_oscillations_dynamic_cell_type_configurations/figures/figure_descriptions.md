# Figure descriptions

Descriptions are based on the PMC/bioRxiv v3 public full text and paired with the extracted main-text PNG files.

## Figure 1

File: `figure_1_behavioral_framework.png`

This figure defines the rule-shift behavior and the trial classification framework. It shows the trial timeline, compound odor-texture cues, IA-to-RS task structure, increased trials-to-criterion during RS, and the strategy classifier that separates stable, exploratory, divergent, and acquisition-related trials.

## Figure 2

File: `figure_2_pvi_interhemispheric_gamma.png`

This figure shows that bilateral PFC PVI gamma synchrony increases specifically after outcomes on strategy-transition trials, especially IA-Divergent and RS-Acquisition trials. It also shows that the same post-outcome increase is not automatically seen in PFC->MD projection neurons.

## Figure 3

File: `figure_3_pfc_md_conflict_gamma.png`

This figure shows conflict-dependent PFC->MD gamma synchrony during decision-making. After mice diverge from the initial association strategy, PFC->MD interhemispheric gamma synchrony becomes higher on non-conflict than conflict trials, while PFC->DS synchrony does not show the same effect.

## Figure 4

File: `figure_4_pvi_opto_inhibition.png`

This figure tests causality by inhibiting PFC PVIs with 638 nm light during the rule-shift task while recording PFC->MD voltage signals. PVI inhibition impairs rule-shift learning, increases perseverative behavior, and disrupts the normal conflict/non-conflict pattern of PFC->MD gamma synchrony.

## Figure 5

File: `figure_5_dual_gevi_configurations.png`

This figure uses dual GEVI recordings to show that PVIs and PFC->MD neurons form behavior-specific synchrony configurations. Non-conflict trials preferentially recruit anti-phase PVI-to-contralateral PFC->MD coupling, whereas conflict trials preferentially recruit anti-phase PVI-to-ipsilateral PFC->MD coupling.
